Coding is essential for the future

Less than 5% of schools taught mathematics during the Industrial Revolution. Widespread unemployment followed until schools added Mathematics to the curriculum.

Today, in the computer age, less than 1% of schools teach coding in early childhood resulting in a major gap in the requirements of the new world and our children’s skills.

In 2020, AI and Robotics will touch 800 Million jobs. Outclass Academy prepares kids in the age group of 8-14 for this new world

Make kids creative, smart and confident

Transformed abstract thinkingYour kids learn decomposition, abstraction, pattern recognition and algorithmic thinking to solve problems logically resulting in significant improvement in abstract thinking scores which is a necessity for scoring top of charts in Math Olympiads and major tests like SAT, JEE, GMAT, CAT etc

Strength and confidenceBeing able to code will help kids stand out in their peer group (until coding becomes as common as Math- which is our vision) and become more confident and generate self-belief to persevere and attain positive outcomes

Merits of early instruction in coding

Develop strong foundations in computational thinking which has applications beyond just software on the back of stronger neural connections in the brain forged at an young age

Learning interventions yield diminishing returns with age based on the work of Harvard psychologist William James

The focus in grades 9-12 shifts to Board exams and entrance exams inhibiting unconstrained learning of computer programming which is possible in early years

What is computational thinking?

Computational thinking is how software engineers solve problems. It combines mathematics, logic and algorithms, and teaches you a new way to think about the world.

Computational thinking teaches you how to tackle large problems by breaking them down into a sequence of smaller, more manageable problems. It allows you to tackle complex problems in efficient ways that operate at huge scale. It involves creating models of the real world with a suitable level of abstraction, and focus on the most pertinent aspects. It helps you go from specific solutions to general ones.

The applications of this approach stretch beyond writing software. Fields as diverse as mechanical engineering, fluid mechanics, physics, biology, archeology and music are applying the computational approach. In business we are beginning to understand that markets often follow rules that can be discerned using computational analysis.

Computational thinking is a skill that everyone should learn. Even if you never become a professional software engineer, you will benefit from knowing how to think this way. It will help you understand and master technology of all sorts and solve problems in almost any discipline.
